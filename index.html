The Project

The Question

What factors most strongly predict whether a student will perform well on their exams? Is it simply how many hours they study, or do deeper factors like parental involvement, access to resources, and sleep play a role?

The Approach

Using Python, I performed a full EDA — cleaning the data, visualising distributions and relationships, and uncovering correlations. I then trained a Random Forest Regressor to predict exam scores and identify the most influential features.

02

Key Findings

📊

Normal Distribution

Exam scores follow a clean bell curve centred around 67, indicating a well-balanced, unbiased dataset with no systematic advantage.

📚

Study Hours Matter

A clear positive correlation (0.45) exists between hours studied and exam scores. However, outliers reveal that effort alone doesn't guarantee success.

🏫

Attendance is King

With the strongest correlation at 0.58, attendance outperforms all other factors. Showing up consistently is the single best predictor of performance.

👨‍👩‍👧

Parental Involvement

Students with higher parental involvement consistently scored higher, though the effect was moderate — suggesting it's a supporting factor, not a primary driver.

😴

Sleep — Surprising Result

Sleep hours showed almost no correlation with exam scores. Medians remained consistent across 4 to 10 hours, suggesting other factors dominate.

🎯

Multi-Factor Reality

No single variable tells the whole story. Outliers throughout the data confirm that human performance is complex, nuanced, and multi-dimensional.
